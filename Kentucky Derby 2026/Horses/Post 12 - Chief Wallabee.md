## Running Style

Mid-to-Backrunner — Chief Wallabee is typically an off-the-pace runner, found in the middle or back of the pack before making his move. However, a notable equipment change for this race — the addition of blinkers for the first time — may signal that connections are looking for a more forwardly placed effort. Blinkers help horses focus and are often added to encourage a sharper break and tighter pace position. How he responds to this change is one of the key storylines to watch on race day.

## Notes

- Trained by Hall of Famer William I. Mott (note: different from Riley Mott, who trains Albus and Incredibolt).
- At 8-1, Chief Wallabee is one of the shorter-priced horses and a legitimate contender.
- The first-time blinker addition is the most interesting wrinkle — if he does rate closer to the pace than expected, it could catch the field off guard.
- Jockey Junior Alvarado is experienced and will need to make real-time decisions about how aggressively to place him early.
- Keep an eye on his post parade behavior — blinkers can sometimes cause horses to be more animated or energetic before the gate opens.
