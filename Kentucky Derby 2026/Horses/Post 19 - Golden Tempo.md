## Running Style

Backrunner/Deep Closer — Golden Tempo has a very well-defined running style and won't deviate from it: he drops far back early and offers one big late run. His connections have shown no inclination to change this approach, making him one of the most predictable horses in the field style-wise. He needs the pace to collapse completely for his late charge to matter, but with four confirmed frontrunners in this field, that scenario is genuinely possible.

## Notes

- Trained by Cherie DeVaux, a notable female trainer with a strong record in top-level races.
- Owned by the prestigious Phipps Stable, one of the most storied names in American thoroughbred racing history.
- Jockey Jose Ortiz is a multiple Eclipse Award winner — an ideal pairing for a deep closer who needs a patient, disciplined ride.
- Post 19 (actual gate 18) is very wide, but for a deep closer it doesn't really matter — he'll be last or near last regardless.
- At 30-1, one of several closers hoping for a pace meltdown. If So Happy, Pavlovian, Six Speed, and Potente all battle for the lead, Golden Tempo could come flying late.
