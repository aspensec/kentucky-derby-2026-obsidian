## Running Style

Stalker/Presser — Further Ado owns the speed to make his own trip and has shown a preference for racing within a few lengths of the pacesetter. He's not a pure frontrunner, but he won't be far off the leaders either. This stalking/pressing style is often considered the ideal Kentucky Derby running position — close enough to react to a tiring leader, but not so far up that he burns energy in a pace duel.

## Notes

- Co-second favorite at 6-1, trained by Brad H. Cox who also trains Commandment (Post 6).
- Cox having two legitimate contenders with completely different running styles gives him an excellent chance regardless of pace shape.
- Jockey John Velazquez is a Hall of Famer and one of the most decorated Derby riders in history — a massive advantage in a race where experience matters.
- Owned by Spendthrift Farm, one of the premier breeding and racing operations in Kentucky — fitting they'd have a contender in America's most famous race.
- Post 18 (actual gate 17) is wide but Velazquez is more than capable of engineering a good trip from anywhere.
- If the pace is hot and the frontrunners tire, Further Ado is perfectly positioned to pounce.
