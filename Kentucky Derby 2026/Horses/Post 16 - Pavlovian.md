## Running Style

Frontrunner — Pavlovian has good early speed and figures to be forcing the tempo right from the break, assuming he gets away cleanly. He'll look to establish the front-running position alongside So Happy, Six Speed, and Potente. The gray's style is pure — he either leads or is near the lead at all times. If the pace duel up front burns out the other speed horses, Pavlovian could theoretically last — but the 1¼-mile distance makes that a tough ask with multiple rivals wanting the same real estate.

## Notes

- Trained by Doug O'Neill, who famously trained I'll Have Another to win the 2012 Kentucky Derby and Preakness.
- Owned by Reddam Racing, the same ownership group behind I'll Have Another — a nostalgic connection for Derby fans.
- The gray coloring makes Pavlovian easy to spot in the race — look for him near the front from the jump.
- Jockey Edwin Maldonado will need to gauge the pace carefully — a four-way battle for the lead could get very fast very early.
- At 30-1, only worth considering if you believe he can steal it on the front end or that the pace somehow doesn't get too hot.
- Post 16 (actual gate 15) is a wide post — he'll need to work hard early to get over if he wants the rail.
