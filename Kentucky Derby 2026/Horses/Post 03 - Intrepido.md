## Running Style

Stalker — Intrepido has shown he is most effective when racing within striking range of the leaders, typically just off the pace. Rather than deep-closing or setting fractions, he stalks the pace and looks to pounce on the leaders in the stretch. His style suits a race where the early speed gets into a battle, allowing him to swoop past tiring front-runners.

## Notes

- A long shot at 50-1, Intrepido would be a massive upset if he won.
- Trainer Jeff Mullins is known for West Coast-based runners; the prep race trail may not have been as competitive as the East Coast route.
- Jockey Hector Berrios will be looking to get a comfortable stalking position early in the race.
- The stalker style is historically one of the more effective at Churchill Downs, which tends to favor horses that save ground and pounce late.
