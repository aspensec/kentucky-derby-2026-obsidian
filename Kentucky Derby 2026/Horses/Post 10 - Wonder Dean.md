## Running Style

Backrunner/Closer — Wonder Dean put in a fine stretch run to collar a leader in his UAE Derby victory, showing a classic closing style that improves as the distance gets longer. He runs particularly well at nine furlongs (1⅛ miles) or longer, which bodes well for the 1¼-mile Kentucky Derby distance. He will drop back early and rely on finding a clear path late to unleash his run.

## Notes

- One of two Japanese Road qualifiers in the field alongside Danon Bourbon (Post 7).
- His UAE Derby win demonstrates he can handle international travel and different racing conditions.
- The longer 1¼-mile Kentucky Derby distance actually plays to his strengths compared to shorter preps.
- Jockey Ryusei Sakai is a highly respected Japanese rider making the trip to Churchill Downs.
- At 30-1, the main question is whether an international shipper can overcome the tactical chaos of a 20-horse American field.
- The Japan Road to the Kentucky Derby has produced increasingly competitive runners in recent years.
