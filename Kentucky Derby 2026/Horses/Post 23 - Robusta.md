## Running Style

Presser/Frontrunner — Robusta has shown a tendency to be forwardly placed, most notably finishing second by a head in the San Felipe after leading in midstretch. He's not a pure wire-to-wire type but he wants to be near the front and applying pressure early. His Santa Anita Derby run was disappointing, finishing seventh, though that race may not have set up favorably for his style. From the widest post in a 23-horse field, he'll need to use his early speed just to find a racing position.

## Notes

- A last-minute addition to the field, drawing in from the also-eligible list after Right to Party was scratched.
- Owned by the legendary Calumet Farm, one of the most storied names in the history of American thoroughbred racing — two-time Kentucky Derby winner with horses like Citation and Whirlaway.
- Trained by Doug O'Neill, who also trains Pavlovian (Post 16) — two horses from the same barn with similar front-running styles, giving O'Neill two shots at the lead.
- Sire is Accelerate, winner of the 2018 Breeders' Cup Classic — a strong stamina pedigree that bodes well for the 1¼-mile distance.
- Post 23 is the widest gate in the field; he'll cover significant extra ground just getting into position early.
- The San Felipe runner-up finish (nearly beating Potente) is the form line that keeps him relevant — he clearly has talent on his best day.
- At 50-1, strictly a longshot, but the Calumet Farm silks are always worth rooting for given the history.
