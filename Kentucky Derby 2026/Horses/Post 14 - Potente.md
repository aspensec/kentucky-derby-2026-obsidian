## Running Style

Frontrunner — Potente has shown high early speed in his races and figures to be forwardly placed from the start, whether sitting just off the pace or outright leading with an alert break from the gate. With an outside post (14, actual gate 13), he'll have room to establish position without getting squeezed. The question is whether he can sustain his speed over 1¼ miles against other speed horses like So Happy, Pavlovian, and Six Speed.

## Notes

- One of two Bob Baffert-trained runners alongside Litmus Test (Post 4) — Baffert enters with two very different running styles.
- Baffert is one of the all-time great Kentucky Derby trainers; his horses are always well-prepared.
- Jockey Juan Hernandez is a capable rider with good experience at Churchill Downs.
- Post 14 (actual gate 13) is a wide outside post — Potente will likely angle in to establish a front-running position, but it will cost him some ground early.
- If the speed battle up front isn't too fierce, a smart front-runner can steal the race wire to wire.
- Horse color listed as Bay based on available information; not officially confirmed in all sources.
