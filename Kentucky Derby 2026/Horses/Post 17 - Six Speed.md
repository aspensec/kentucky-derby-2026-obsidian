## Running Style

Frontrunner — Six Speed was clear in second after dictating the tempo in the UAE Derby and will be forwardly placed from the break here. He's a Euro/Middle East Road qualifier who loves setting or tracking the early pace. From the far outside Post 17 (actual gate 16), his early speed should allow him to find a comfortable position near the front, though he'll cover extra ground getting there.

## Notes

- Euro/Middle East Road qualifier, making him one of three international runners alongside the two Japanese horses.
- Trainer Bhupat Seemar is a Dubai-based trainer with significant experience in international racing.
- Jockey Brian Hernandez Jr. is a Kentucky-based rider very familiar with Churchill Downs — a real advantage for an international runner.
- Post 17 (actual gate 16) is one of the widest posts in the field; the horse will need to use his early speed to find a comfortable position quickly.
- At 40-1, the longest shot among the frontrunners — going up against So Happy, Pavlovian, and Potente for the early lead is a tough ask.
