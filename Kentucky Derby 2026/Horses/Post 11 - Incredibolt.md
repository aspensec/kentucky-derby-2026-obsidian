## Running Style

Stalker/Midrunner — Incredibolt likes to sit just behind the front runners to conserve energy before making his challenge in the stretch. This patient stalking style conserves energy through the early fractions and positions him to pick off tiring leaders in the final stages. It's a classic "track the pace" approach — not as risky as deep closing, and can be very effective if he gets a good setup.

## Notes

- Trained by Riley Mott, who also trains Albus (Post 2) — two horses from the same barn with slightly different running styles.
- Both Incredibolt and Albus are owned by Pin Oak Stud (Dana Bernhard), making this one of the dual-entry barns to watch.
- Jockey Jaime Torres will look to find a comfortable mid-pack stalking spot from Post 11.
- Post 11 is a reasonable gate position — not too far outside to waste ground, but enough room to maneuver early.
- At 20-1, could be the better bet of the two Mott/Pin Oak horses given his more conservative running style.
